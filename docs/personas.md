## The Beginner

**Age 19–28**

### Who They Are
A young man who has never had a skincare or grooming routine. Uses 3-in-1 body wash on his face. Wants to look better but doesn't know where to start. Found FORGE through social media (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts). Likely single or early in a relationship. Works an entry-level job or is in university. His current "routine" is whatever his roommate left in the shower.

### Goals
- Get clear skin and stop breakouts
- Learn basic grooming without feeling overwhelmed
- Build a simple daily routine he can actually maintain
- Look better in photos (especially dating app profile shots)
- Feel more confident approaching women or in social situations
- See visible progress to stay motivated

### Pain Points Without This Platform
- Overwhelmed by the sheer number of products (serums? toners? retinoids?)
- Doesn't trust influencer advice — feels like paid shilling
- Tried and failed before (bought products, used them for 3 days, gave up)
- Doesn't have much time — max 15 minutes per day for grooming tasks
- Self-conscious about his appearance but doesn't want to admit he cares
- Has no vocabulary for grooming — doesn't know what "exfoliate" or "moisturise" means
- Can't tell if anything is working — no baseline, no measurement
- Feels like grooming is feminine or vain, needs permission to care about it

### Technical Comfort
Moderate. Uses apps daily (social media, dating, fitness). Comfortable with subscriptions. Expects everything to work seamlessly — will uninstall if onboarding takes more than 5 minutes. Doesn't read documentation. Needs visual, immediate feedback. Responds well to gamification (XP, streaks, badges).

### Key Behaviours
- Opens app in morning while getting ready
- Skips tasks when busy but comes back later in the day
- Takes progress photos every 3 days consistently (reminded by push notification)
- Shares progress card publicly if he sees visible improvement — this is the viral loop
- Never misses a weekly report — the Sunday summary is his anchor
- Prefers outcome titles ("Clearer skin within 30 days") over clinical names
- Needs early wins (First Strike celebration, quick XP gains) to build momentum
- Gets discouraged if he falls behind — guilt-free re-engagement is critical

## The Optimiser

**Age 28–40**

### Who They Are
An established professional who already has basic grooming habits. Uses some products (moisturiser, maybe retinol). Has a barber he sees regularly. Wants to go from "decent" to "sharp." Values data-driven improvement and evidence over marketing claims. Found FORGE through a recommendation (friend, colleague, or algorithmic discovery). Likely in a relationship or married. Reads about biohacking, watches Huberman Lab clips, owns a fitness tracker.

### Goals
- Quantifiable appearance improvement — he wants numbers, not feelings
- Optimised routine specific to his face shape (knows one-size-fits-all doesn't work)
- Understand the WHY behind every product and habit — mechanism language
- Visible jawline improvement (facial composition)
- Style upgrade that fits his frame and body type
- Maximise his appearance within his time budget (30 min/day max)
- Track progress over time with data, not selfies alone

### Pain Points Without This Platform
- Plateaued with his current routine — doing the basics but not improving
- Doesn't know what to change next — no guided progression
- No objective measurement of progress — mirror-gazing is unreliable
- Busy schedule — career, relationship, maybe kids — can't spend hours on grooming
- Skeptical of marketing claims and influencer culture — needs evidence
- Has tried multiple products without a system — waste of money
- Wants to optimise but doesn't know which levers to pull
- Doesn't want to become a "grooming guy" — wants efficient, effective, evidence-backed

### Technical Comfort
High. Uses productivity apps (Notion, Todoist), quantified-self tools (Whoop, Oura, Apple Health), and financial trackers. Expects data visualisation, trend graphs, and transparent methodology. Will read the "why it works" section of every task. Appreciates the mechanism-level language (days 31+). May want data export. Judges the product on the quality of its insights, not its gamification.

### Key Behaviours
- Opens app first thing in the morning as part of his routine stack
- Studies AI insights carefully — reads the full weekly report, not just the score
- Compares cycle photos side-by-side looking for subtle differences
- Tracks pillar scores over time — notices when a pillar is stagnant
- Shares insights with friends ("did you know posture affects perceived dominance?"), not just before/after photos
- Completes all daily tasks consistently — streak is important but as a habit metric, not a game
- May be a power user who requests features (data export, custom plan regeneration)
- Values the season report deeply — wants to see the full journey in data

## The Returning User

**Day 30+ in-program, returning after 1–2 weeks away**

### Who They Are
Someone who completed 30+ days of FORGE, built momentum, then stopped for 1–2 weeks. Coming back. Life got in the way — work crunch, travel, illness, or just fell off the wagon. Might feel guilty about breaking their streak. Might have been avoiding opening the app out of shame. This persona is not demographic — it's behavioural. Any user can become a Returning User at any point in their journey.

### Goals
- Get back on track without punishment or shaming
- Pick up where they left off — not restart from day 1
- Preserve their progress data (scores, photos, achievements)
- Rebuild momentum quickly with an easy win
- Understand what changed while they were away
- Feel welcomed back, not judged

### Pain Points Without This Platform
- Feels bad about breaking the streak — the number going to zero stings
- Worried the program considers them a "failure" — might avoid opening the app
- Unsure if their plan is still valid or if they need to restart
- Doesn't want to see a guilt-trip notification (FORGE policy: no guilt language)
- Concerned that missed days permanently damaged their progress
- May have "failed" previous self-improvement attempts — this feels like another one
- Needs the re-entry friction to be zero — every extra tap is a chance to close the app

### Technical Comfort
Already familiar with the app. Knows the navigation, understands the scoring system, has completed multiple cycle check-ins. Doesn't need onboarding or education. Just needs resumption. Any re-education should be subtle and contextual, not a forced tutorial.

### Key Behaviours
- Opens app cautiously — expects to see their old data preserved
- Resumes from their last program day, not day 1 (program day advancement logic preserves progress)
- Gets a supportive daily insight, not a guilt trip ("Good to see you. Skin pillar held steady." — not "You missed 12 days.")
- Sees their cycle photos still there — proof their journey wasn't erased
- Checks their streak first — this is the emotional barrier
- May need one easy task to rebuild confidence (low-duration, high-visible-impact task)
- Returns fully after confirming nothing was lost — data preservation is the trust anchor
- May become a more loyal user after returning successfully — the comeback story is powerful
